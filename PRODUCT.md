# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Workers (Freelancers)**
Context: applying to be vetted talent, waiting on admin review, checking introduction status.
Job: get credibly introduced to real clients through a trusted intermediary — not just another profile on a crowded platform.

**Clients (Business Buyers)**
Context: reviewing curated talent shortlists, not browsing self-serve catalogues.
Job: find pre-verified freelancers fast, without sifting through noise.

**Admins (QuickQuid Team)**
Context: manually triaging worker applications, creating matches, triggering introductions.
Job: make high-confidence, irreversible decisions that unlock value for both sides.

## Product Purpose

QuickQuid is a trust-first, human-verified freelance marketplace connecting vetted freelance talent with business clients. The core mechanic is the "Introduce" action — an irreversible, admin-triggered event that unlocks a worker's contact data to a specific client. No automated matching, no self-serve browsing, no payment processing. QuickQuid is the "Silent Coordinator": it verifies, curates, and introduces — then steps aside.

Success looks like: clients trust that every worker they see has been personally reviewed; workers trust that introductions are meaningful, not spam; admins have a clear, auditable workflow for every decision they make.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, Premium, Human.

Voice: calm authority, not hype. Copy is direct and specific — never vague or startup-breathless. The product earns trust through clarity, not claims.

Emotional goal: clients should feel confident (not overwhelmed); workers should feel respected (not like applicants on a conveyor belt); admins should feel empowered (not buried in tooling).

## Anti-references

- **Generic LinkedIn clone**: No resume walls, connection counts, or self-serve networking energy. QuickQuid is curated — the admin is always in the room.
- **Startup-bro tech aesthetic**: No purple gradients, "disrupting X" hero copy, bold/aggressive color, or crypto-adjacent UI patterns.
- **Government / enterprise legacy**: No gray data tables, dense 10px form labels, or clunky modal flows. The admin experience should feel like a well-designed tool, not a back-office system.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust is visible** — Every surface signals curation. Verified badges, admin notes, introduced-at timestamps are not decoration — they are the product.
2. **Irreversibility deserves ceremony** — The "Introduce" action is permanent. Its UI should reflect that weight: confirmation dialogs, clear language, no accidental triggers.
3. **Role-appropriate context** — Workers see their journey. Clients see their matches. Admins see the full picture. No role sees what isn't theirs.
4. **Clarity over density** — Prefer whitespace and hierarchy over data tables and badge counts. One primary action per screen. Signal over noise.
5. **Human over algorithmic** — Admin decisions are deliberate, not automated. UI should support considered action, not speed-clicking.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large/bold text. Full keyboard navigation. Screen reader support via semantic HTML and aria-labels on icon-only controls. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all transitions.
